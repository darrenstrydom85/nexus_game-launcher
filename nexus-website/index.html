In motion

See Nexus in action.

Three signature moments from the launcher.

Stat 01

Track every minute. Spot every pattern.

Your sessions flow into a dashboard built for inspection — heatmaps, histograms, return rate, and per-game breakdowns by month or weekday.

  • Daily, weekly, and all-time totals across every storefront
  • Activity heatmap and play-time-by-day-of-week charts
  • Session length distribution with mean, median, and p75
  • Per-game pattern view with return-rate and gap analysis

Stat 02

Your year, wrapped — and shareable.

A cinematic year-in-review built right into your library. Top games, genres, mood, hidden gems, and a 1080×1080 poster to drop in any feed.

  • Top-3 podium for your most-played games
  • Genre breakdown, mood tagline, and hidden-gem spotlight
  • Custom date ranges — month, year, or anything between
  • Export shareable poster card straight to clipboard

Stat 03

See what's trending. Catch streamers live.

Twitch is woven through the launcher — pulse badges on game cards, live streams in the sidebar, trending titles you already own, and go-live toasts.

  • Live and offline view of every channel you follow
  • Trending in your library — your games, ranked by viewers
  • Pulse badges on game cards, click to jump straight to a stream
  • Favourites pinning + go-live toast notifications

Under the hood

Built for people who care how things work.

A native Rust core wrapped in a React UI. Tiny install, instant launch, and your data on your disk — exactly where it belongs.

> Why it's fast: the core runs as a native Rust binary, not Electron.
> Why it's small: Windows ships WebView2 — Nexus reuses it instead of bundling Chromium.
> Why it's private: no servers, no accounts, no analytics in the desktop app. Your library lives in a single SQLite file you control.
> Why it's open: every release is documented in a public changelog with versioned MSI downloads.

Latest release

v0.3.2

Apr 23, 2026 Live now

A complete Twitch overhaul. Watch live streams without leaving Nexus, get push-based go-live alerts the moment a streamer you follow starts broadcasting, browse the top clips for any game in your library, and see your Twitch watch time roll into your Stats and Wrapped. Sign-in is more reliable, and there is a brand-new diagnostics panel if anything ever feels off.

  • In-app Twitch stream viewer
  • Push go-live alerts (EventSub)
  • Top clips on every game page
  • Twitch watch time in Stats & Wrapped
  • Self-healing token refresh
  • Twitch diagnostics panel
